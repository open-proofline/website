# Governance And Political Alignment

Proofline is experimental public-interest open-source infrastructure. It is
being built for records that should not disappear just because a device does,
while keeping access explicit, bounded, and user-controlled.

This document is the canonical project-level source for Proofline's public
governance posture and political alignment. Repository-specific READMEs should
link here instead of rewriting this posture differently in every corner of the
project.

## Current Status

Proofline is currently maintainer-led. It does not currently claim to be an
incorporated cooperative, registered charity, registered nonprofit, foundation,
company, or staffed governance body.

That caveat matters. Public-good language should describe the project's
direction and commitments, not pretend a legal structure already exists.

Proofline is experimental and not production-ready emergency infrastructure. It
is not an emergency service, emergency dispatch system, emergency-services
integration, staffed response center, or guaranteed real-time response system.

## Political Alignment

Proofline is aligned with cooperative and libertarian socialist principles:
workplace democracy, shared accountability, public-good infrastructure,
transparent worker compensation, and pay for labour rather than ownership
extraction.

Proofline's politics are not meant to be a sticker on the README. They should
shape the project structure, the funding model, the deployment posture, and the
technical choices that make future project capture harder.

## Planned Governance Direction

The planned long-term direction is a non-distributing cooperative or similar
mission-locked public-good structure. If the project reaches that stage,
governance should make mission drift, vendor lock-in, investor capture, and
private extraction harder.

Future governance should support:

- workplace democracy
- shared accountability
- transparent worker compensation
- public-interest decision-making
- user control and self-hosting
- resistance to surveillance and platform dependency
- a clear boundary between paid work and ownership extraction

Proofline should not quietly mutate into a trust-branded SaaS funnel. If the
structure cannot resist that, the structure is part of the security problem.

## Paid Work, Not Ownership Extraction

Founder or director compensation, if the project can support it, should be
compensation for defined work performed for Proofline. It should not be founder
equity, dividends, ownership returns, private profit distribution, or extraction
because someone arrived early.

Paying people for real labour is compatible with public-good infrastructure.
Letting ownership claims drain the project later is not.

## Surplus Reinvestment

Surplus, if any exists, should be reinvested into Proofline and aligned
public-good work.

Appropriate reinvestment may include:

- Proofline development
- security reviews and audits
- infrastructure
- documentation
- support
- contributor compensation
- public-good deployments
- related open-source tooling
- smaller aligned upstream or open-source projects

The point is to keep value circulating through the commons Proofline depends on
and stands beside, not to turn user trust into private yield.

## Open Source, Self-Hosting, And Anti-Capture

Self-hosting and open source are part of resisting platform dependency and
surveillance-capitalism gravity. They are not decorative values.

Proofline should resist:

- vendor lock-in
- investor capture
- proprietary dependency traps
- community-washed SaaS mutation
- governance structures that allow trust to be sold later with a nicer logo

This does not mean every deployment will be identical or that future hosted
service cost recovery is forbidden. It means the project's structure should not
make public trust subordinate to private exit paths.

## Funding Boundaries

Funding, donations, and any future official hosted-service cost recovery must
not be treated as:

- emergency assistance
- support priority
- ownership
- investment return
- tax-deductible charity status
- proof of production readiness
- feature access unless a future implemented and documented product flow says so

Donations do not create accounts, unlock features, provide support priority, or
provide emergency assistance. Future billing, if it exists, should recover
hosting and maintenance costs rather than turn Proofline into a profit-maximizing
service wearing a public-good hat.

## Legal And Status Caveat

This document is not legal advice, bylaws, a constitution, an incorporation
statement, an investment document, or a claim that a cooperative already legally
exists.

It is a public source-of-truth document for the project's intended governance
direction and political alignment. If later legal documents are created, they
should be reviewed on their own terms and kept consistent with this public
posture.
